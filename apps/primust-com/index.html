A Signed, Portable Proof That Your Checks Ran

A VPEC is a cryptographically signed JSON credential that proves a specific set of governance checks executed against specific inputs and produced specific outputs. It contains commitment hashes — never raw content — so the credential is portable, shareable, and verifiable by anyone without exposing the underlying data.

Think of it as a receipt for governance. A PDF report says "we checked." A VPEC proves it — mathematically, with a timestamp anchor, offline-verifiable forever. No trust in Primust required to verify one.

Portable
JSON. Attach to any artifact, store anywhere, forward across org boundaries.
Offline-Verifiable
Verify with a single public key fetch. No network calls after that. Works forever.
Content-Blind
Only cryptographic hashes. The credential proves execution without revealing what was checked.
01How It Works
Input → Checks → Output → Verify
You run whatever checks you run. We make the cryptographic proof that they ran. Content never transits Primust — only hashes reach the signing endpoint.
01
Define
Declare what checks your process runs. Use our policy bundles or bring your own policy engine — OPA, Cedar, Drools.
02
Instrument
Two lines of code. The SDK captures check inputs, outputs, and timing. Zero content leaves your environment — only cryptographic hashes reach the signing endpoint.
03
Sign
Hashes are signed with tamper-evident keys and anchored to a trusted timestamp. Enterprise customers sign with their own keys — Primust never holds the trust anchor.
04
Verify
Anyone verifies offline, forever. No account needed. No network calls. The verifier is Apache-2.0, free forever. Works even if Primust disappears.
